# FAL — Fantasy Auction League: Player Guide

**Your private IPL fantasy league. Built for people who take cricket seriously.**

---

## What is FAL?

FAL is a private fantasy cricket league for IPL. You and your friends each build a squad in a pre-season auction, then compete over the IPL season by picking the best XI from your squad each week.

Unlike Dream11 where everyone starts fresh each gameweek, **your squad is yours for the entire season.** The players you win at auction are your players — for better or worse. That makes every selection matter.

---

## How It Works

### Before the Season — The Auction
Before IPL begins, every manager in your league bids on real IPL players. The admin runs the auction and uploads the final rosters. Once squads are set, the season begins.

Your squad will have **12–15 players**. You can't trade or sign new players (that's coming in a future season).

### Every Gameweek — Pick Your Best XI
Each gameweek (Monday to Sunday), you pick:
- **11 players** from your squad to play
- **1 Captain** — earns double points
- **1 Vice Captain** — earns double points only if your Captain doesn't play at all that GW
- **Bench order** (1–4) — your backup if someone doesn't play
- **A strategy chip** (optional — see below)

**Deadline:** Your lineup locks the moment the first IPL match of the week kicks off. Miss it and your previous week's lineup carries forward.

### Automatic Cover — Bench Substitutions
If one of your Playing XI doesn't feature in any match that week, the system automatically brings in your next available bench player. Your bench order matters — set it carefully.

## Points System

Points are earned from real IPL performance. Here's what scores:

### Batting
| Action | Points |
|---|---|
| Each run | +1 |
| Four | +4 bonus |
| Six | +6 bonus |
| 25-run milestone | +4 |
| Half-century (50) | +8 |
| 75-run milestone | +12 |
| Century (100) | +16 *(replaces all lower milestones)* |
| Duck (0 runs, batters/keepers/all-rounders only) | −2 |

**Strike rate also counts** (minimum 10 balls faced; bowlers are exempt from SR penalties):
| Strike Rate | Points |
|---|---|
| 170+ | +6 |
| 150–170 | +4 |
| 130–150 | +2 |
| 70–130 | 0 |
| 60–70 | −2 |
| 50–60 | −4 |
| Below 50 | −6 |

### Bowling
| Action | Points |
|---|---|
| Dot ball | +1 |
| Wicket | +30 |
| LBW or Bowled bonus | +8 |
| Maiden over | +12 |
| 3-wicket haul bonus | +4 |
| 4-wicket haul bonus | +8 |
| 5-wicket haul bonus | +12 |

**Economy rate also counts** (minimum 2 overs bowled):
| Economy Rate | Points |
|---|---|
| Below 5.00 | +6 |
| 5–6 | +4 |
| 6–7 | +2 |
| 7–10 | 0 |
| 10–11 | −2 |
| 11–12 | −4 |

### Fielding
| Action | Points |
|---|---|
| Catch | +8 |
| 3 catches in a match | +4 bonus |
| Stumping | +12 |
| Run out (you alone) | +12 |
| Run out (assisted) | +6 each |

### Appearing
| Action | Points |
|---|---|
| Named in Starting XI | +4 |
| Coming on as Impact Player sub | +4 |

### Captain & Vice Captain
Your Captain earns **2×** all their points for the week — always.

Your Vice Captain earns **2× only if your Captain does not play** at all that gameweek — not in the starting XI and not as an Impact Sub in any match. If your Captain plays even one match, your VC earns normal points with no multiplier.

> **Example (Captain plays):** Captain scores 80 pts → you get 160. VC scores 60 pts → you get 60.
> **Example (Captain absent):** Captain scores 0 (didn't play) → VC steps up to 2×. VC scores 60 pts → you get 120.

## Multi-Match Gameweeks

Some players play **two or three matches** in a single gameweek. Their points accumulate across all matches before your Captain/VC multiplier is applied.

> **This is huge.** A player with two games this week could score twice the points. Look for these fixtures when picking your Captain.

## Strategy Chips

You have **2 chips** for the entire season — one of each. Each can only be used **once** and only **one per gameweek**. Use them wisely — they can swing a gameweek dramatically.

---

### 🎳 Bowling Boost
**All your bowlers earn 2× points this gameweek.**

Best used in a week where your bowling-heavy players face batting-friendly pitches and you expect big performances. Stack with a bowling Captain for up to 4× on your Captain's points.

*Best used when:* You have 4–5 elite bowlers playing this week, ideally in matches where wickets are likely.

---

### 🏏 Power Play Bat
**All your batters earn 2× points this gameweek.**

The mirror of Bowling Boost — if your squad is stacked with top-order batters who have great fixtures this week, this can be devastating. Most squads have more batters than bowlers, so this chip often has a higher ceiling.

*Best used when:* Your batters have flat-pitch fixtures and you have a double or triple gameweek batter to Captain.

---

### Chip Strategy Tips

- **Don't panic-use chips early.** The best weeks are double or triple gameweek weeks — a player with two matches under a chip doubles your ceiling.
- **Power Play Bat + batting Captain = 4× on your Captain.** Stack these when you have a top-order batter captained in a great fixture.
- **Bowling Boost + bowling Captain = 4× on your Captain.** Save this for a week where your best bowler has two matches or a wicket-friendly pitch.
- **You only have one of each.** Once a chip is played it's gone for the season — the app will ask you to confirm before activating.
- **The chips are roughly symmetric** — Power Play Bat benefits more players in a typical XI (batters outnumber bowlers), so pick your Bowling Boost week carefully for maximum impact.

## Leaderboard & Winning

The season leaderboard ranks managers by **total points across all gameweeks**. If two managers are tied, the one with the higher single-gameweek score wins the tiebreaker.

Scores update live as each IPL match finishes — you can watch your gameweek total climb in real time.

---

## Quick Tips

1. **Captain selection is everything.** Half your strategy each week is picking the right Captain — prioritise players with double fixtures.

2. **Check fixture lists before picking your XI.** A player with two games this week is almost always worth picking over someone with one game, even if the single-game player is slightly better on paper.

3. **Set your bench order deliberately.** It's not just a formality — your bench order determines who covers XI gaps. And remember: your VC only earns a multiplier if your Captain is absent, so Captain selection carries all the weight.

4. **Don't save chips too long.** There's a temptation to hoard chips for the perfect week that never comes. Double-gameweek weeks are your trigger.

5. **Economy matters as much as wickets.** A bowler going at 12+ economy loses 6 points. A wicket-taking bowler who's expensive can hurt you.
